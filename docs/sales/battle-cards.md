# TripOS Competitor Battle Cards

Internal sales reference. Use in demos, discovery calls, WhatsApp and email.
Rule: **never bash a competitor.** Be fair, then reframe to where we win.
Honesty contract: we are pre-launch — **no fabricated customer counts, no fake
reviews.** Sell the product's capability and the India-native fit, not traction
we don't have yet.

---

## The real competitor: the patchwork (WhatsApp + Excel + Canva + Word)

> **90% of our prospects are not on any software.** This is the deal we win most.

- **When we win:** Always, if they feel the pain. The patchwork is free but costs hours per proposal, leaks leads, and looks unprofessional.
- **Talk track:** *"You're not really choosing between us and another tool — you're choosing between half a day in Word + Canva + Excel, and 10 minutes in one place. What does one lost lead cost you?"*
- **Kill shot:** Build their actual next proposal live in the demo. The time-collapse from hours → minutes closes it.
- **Objection — "we're fine on WhatsApp/Excel":** *"You are, until a lead goes cold or a client says the proposal looked basic. How many enquiries last month never got a follow-up?"*

## Sembark

- **One-liner:** Established Indian tour-operator software, stronger for larger operators / back-office & inventory depth.
- **Their strengths:** Brand recognition in India, established, broader back-office/operations feature set, real review presence.
- **Their weaknesses (where we win):** Not AI-first; proposal/itinerary experience less of a wow; heavier to set up; less oriented to the 1–5 person owner-led agency.
- **When WE win:** Small / owner-led agency that lives on proposals + WhatsApp and wants AI + beautiful proposals + self-serve setup.
- **When THEY win:** Larger operator whose primary need is deep inventory/contracting and back-office.
- **Landmine to plant (fair):** *"Ask how fast you can send your first proposal yourself, without an onboarding call."*
- **Kill shot:** AI itinerary → branded proposal → WhatsApp send, in under 10 minutes, in the demo.
- **Objection — "Sembark is more established":** *"True — and great for bigger operators. We built tripOS for agencies your size that want to look premium and move fast, not run an enterprise rollout. Try both free; you'll feel the difference in the first proposal."*

## helloGTX

- **One-liner:** Long-standing travel CRM, oriented to B2B / larger travel businesses.
- **Their strengths:** Maturity, B2B/agent-network features, established in the trade.
- **Their weaknesses:** Dated UX, enterprise setup footprint, not AI-first, not self-serve.
- **When WE win:** Small/owner-led agency wanting modern, AI-first, all-in-one with transparent pricing.
- **When THEY win:** Large B2B travel business needing a heavy CRM rollout.
- **Kill shot:** "Start today" — free trial + self-serve setup vs a sales-and-onboarding cycle.
- **Objection — "they do more":** *"More modules you'll never configure. We do the 6 things that make you money, beautifully, and you're live today."*

## CRMTravel

- **One-liner:** A travel CRM option; weaker brand, product polish and content presence.
- **Their strengths:** Travel-specific, lower-priced positioning.
- **Their weaknesses:** Not AI-first; thinner proposal experience; weak modern UX.
- **When WE win:** Almost any prospect who values proposal quality, AI and a modern interface.
- **Kill shot:** Side-by-side proposal output — ours looks like a luxury brand's.
- **Objection — "it's cheaper":** *"Price is easy to match; a proposal that wins the booking pays for itself once. What's a single lost trip worth?"*

## Travefy

- **One-liner:** Popular US itinerary + proposal tool. Good product, wrong market for India.
- **Their strengths:** Polished itineraries/proposals, strong brand in the US.
- **Their weaknesses (in India):** No GST tax invoicing, no INR-first billing or Indian gateways, email-first not WhatsApp-first, USD pricing.
- **When WE win:** Any India-based agency — automatically, on GST + INR + WhatsApp.
- **When THEY win:** US-based agency needing only itineraries/proposals.
- **Kill shot:** *"Travefy can't issue your GST invoice or take an INR payment. We do the whole India workflow."*

## Tourwriter

- **One-liner:** Established global tour-operator software, enterprise-leaning.
- **Their strengths:** Deep itinerary/contracting features for large operators.
- **Their weaknesses (in India):** Not India-native (no GST/INR), email-first, heavier setup, higher cost.
- **When WE win:** Small/owner-led Indian agency wanting simple, affordable, AI-first, India-native.
- **Kill shot:** AI speed + India-native billing + transparent ₹2,499 pricing vs enterprise setup.

## Zoho CRM

- **One-liner:** Excellent general CRM, not built for travel.
- **Their strengths:** Cheap, flexible, vast ecosystem, brand trust.
- **Their weaknesses (for travel):** No AI itineraries, no travel proposals, no GST travel invoicing, no vouchers — you bolt on 4 tools and weeks of config.
- **When WE win:** Any agency that tried to force-fit Zoho to travel. **Best inbound trigger we have.**
- **Kill shot:** *"You spent 3 weeks configuring Zoho and still can't send an itinerary or a GST invoice. tripOS does it on day one."*
- **Objection — "we already pay for Zoho":** *"Keep it for non-travel if you like — but your trips, proposals, GST and vouchers belong in a tool built for travel. That's where the time and lost leads are."*

## HubSpot

- **One-liner:** Premium general CRM/marketing platform; overkill and off-target for a small travel agency.
- **Their strengths:** Best-in-class general CRM/marketing, brand.
- **Their weaknesses (for travel):** Expensive at scale, not travel-native (same gaps as Zoho), heavy.
- **When WE win:** Travel agency that doesn't need a marketing-automation suite — it needs to send proposals and invoices.
- **Kill shot:** *"HubSpot is built to run marketing for any company. tripOS is built to run a travel agency."*

## Universal demo close (any competitor)

1. Confirm their current workflow + biggest pain (60s).
2. The wow: their real enquiry → AI itinerary → branded proposal → WhatsApp send (5 min).
3. Pipeline + follow-up so no lead is lost (3 min).
4. GST invoice + INR payment — the India trust closer (3 min).
5. Pricing + founding offer scarcity (real: "X of 100 lifetime spots left, closes 31 Jul 2026") → start trial (3 min).

## Universal positioning line (say it every call)

> "tripOS is the operating system for Indian travel agencies — it turns an enquiry into a branded proposal, a paid booking and a GST invoice, in one flow."
