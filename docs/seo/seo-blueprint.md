```
│     └── [build] /tutterfly-alternative, vs-pages (tripos-vs-X)
│
├── PILLAR C: Guides & blog   /blog   (TRAFFIC + AUTHORITY → links down to A & B)
│     ├── Destination playbooks (programmatic, see §4)
│     ├── How-to / workflow guides

│

```

**Rule:** every page links *up* to its pillar and *across* to 1–2 siblings;
pillars link *down* to spokes; blog posts link *into* Pillar A/B money pages.
Home links to the three pillar hubs. This concentrates authority on the money
pages and is exactly what AI crawlers traverse.

> ⚠️ Programmatic guardrails: each page needs **genuinely unique, useful copy**
> (not spun templates) or Google treats it as thin/doorway content. The
> comparison pages are the quality bar — match it. Build city/segment pages only
> where you can say something real (local GST, local agent context).

# 5. Internal-linking rules (enforce on every page)
- **Up:** every spoke → its pillar hub (e.g. `/travel-agency-crm` → `/travel-agency-software-india`). (Already in `related[]`.)
- **Across:** 1–2 sibling spokes (CRM ↔ proposals ↔ GST).
- **Down:** pillar hubs → all their spokes.
- **Blog → money:** every post links to ≥1 pillar/spoke with keyword-rich anchor (done in the 3 new posts).
- **Comparison → comparison:** alternative pages cross-link (done) + link to `/best-travel-agency-software-india` hub.
- **Money → proof + pricing:** every money page links to `/pricing` and (later) case studies.
- **Anchor text:** use the target page's head keyword as anchor, vary slightly to avoid exact-match over-optimisation.
