# TripOS Partnerships Engine (Phase 5)

On near-zero budget, partnerships are the highest-leverage channel: someone else
already has your exact audience (Indian travel-agency owners). Borrow their trust.

**Priority order:** Trainers/Educators → SaaS integration partners → Communities/Associations → Influencers. (Trainers first: they have warm, buying audiences and a reason to recommend a tool their students will need.)

---

## 1. Travel trainers / educators (HIGHEST PRIORITY)
**Who:** People who run "Start your travel agency / become a travel agent" courses, bootcamps and YouTube channels in India. Their students are your exact ICP at the exact moment of need.
**Why they matter:** Their students *will* need software — be the recommended one, bundled into the course.
**Find them:** YouTube ("how to start a travel agency in India"), Instagram travel-educator hashtags, Udemy/local course platforms, LinkedIn.
**Outreach:** "Your students ask what software to use, right? I'll give your cohort a free extended trial + a founding discount, set them up live, and pay you {20–30%} recurring for anyone who subscribes. Want to pilot with your next batch?"
**Structure:** Affiliate / revenue-share. Unique coupon + link per trainer. Recurring share (not one-time) so they keep promoting.
**Revenue-share model:** 20–30% recurring for 12 months, OR a flat ₹/signup + a co-branded onboarding webinar. Offer their students an exclusive extended trial (not a discount that breaks founding integrity).
**Expected impact:** 🔴 High. One active trainer with a 100-student batch ≈ 10–25 trials. Compounds per cohort.

## 2. SaaS / integration partners
**Who & why:**
- **Razorpay (Partner Program):** you already use it — join their partner directory; co-market "payments for travel agencies." Listing = backlink + referrals.
- **WhatsApp BSPs (Gupshup / Wati / Interakt):** you use WhatsApp Cloud API — co-marketing to their travel-agency users; integration story.
- **Tally / Zoho Books:** accounting integration removes a switching objection ("does it work with my CA?"). Listing in their marketplace = discovery.
- **Google (Business Profile / Workspace):** you already have Drive/Gmail integration — get into relevant directories.
**Outreach:** "We send Indian travel agencies to {Razorpay/WhatsApp}; let's co-market / list each other." Lead with the traffic you'll send them.
**Structure:** Mutual listing + co-marketing (webinar, blog swap, directory). Usually no money changes hands — it's distribution + backlinks (also AI-citation sources, ties to `docs/sales/battle-cards.md` and Phase 1).
**Expected impact:** 🟡 Medium direct signups, 🟢 High for backlinks/credibility/AI-discoverability.

## 3. Travel associations
**Who:** TAAI, TAFI, IATO, OTOAI, ADTOI, and state/city travel-agent associations.
**Why:** Instant credibility + access to thousands of member agencies; speaking slots; member newsletters.
**Outreach:** Offer a **member-exclusive deal** (extended trial + onboarding), a free educational webinar ("Modernise your agency / GST & proposals masterclass"), or sponsor a local chapter event cheaply.
**Structure:** Member benefit + sponsorship/speaking. Association promotes; you give members a perk.
**Expected impact:** 🟡 Medium–High; slower (relationship-driven) but high-trust and bulk reach. Great for Persona C.

## 4. Influencers / travel-agent finfluencers
**Who:** Instagram/YouTube creators whose audience is travel *agents* (not travellers) — agency coaches, "travelpreneur" accounts.
**Why:** Reach + social proof to Persona A/B where they already scroll.
**Outreach:** Free account + affiliate link; ask for an honest "tools I use / would recommend" feature. Prefer authentic usage over paid ads.
**Structure:** Affiliate (same as trainers) + occasional paid collab once you have budget/proof.
**Expected impact:** 🟡 Medium; spiky. Best paired with the founding offer and a strong Reel (ties to `docs/marketing/distribution-playbook.md`).

## 5. Travel consultants / sub-agent networks & DMCs
**Who:** DMCs and B2B suppliers whose reseller agents would sell *their* packages faster with good software; large agencies with sub-agent networks.
**Why:** Aligned incentive — a DMC wants its agents to look professional and close faster. Seeds the future **agent↔supplier network effect** (the long-term moat).
**Outreach:** "Your reseller agents close more when their proposals look premium and go out fast. Let's offer tripOS to your network — co-branded, with a group rate."
**Structure:** Bulk/white-label-ish group deal or referral; co-branded onboarding.
**Expected impact:** 🟢 High long-term (network seed), 🟡 medium short-term.

---

## Partner program — the standard offer (keep it simple)
- **Recurring revenue share:** 20–30% for 12 months per referred paying agency.
- **Tracking:** unique coupon + UTM link per partner.
- **Partner gets:** co-branded onboarding webinar, a simple asset kit (Reel, carousel, email copy — pull from distribution playbook), their students/members get an exclusive *extended trial* (protect founding-price integrity; don't undercut it).
- **You get:** warm, pre-trusted signups at near-zero CAC + backlinks/AI-citations.

## Weekly partnership cadence (founder)
1 new partner conversation/week · activate/support existing partners · ship 1 co-marketing asset. Track: partners live, signups per partner, recurring revenue attributed.
